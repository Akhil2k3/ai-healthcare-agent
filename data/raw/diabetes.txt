Diabetes is a chronic health condition that affects how the body turns food into energy.

Type 2 diabetes is the most common form. It occurs when the body becomes resistant to insulin or does not produce enough insulin.

Common symptoms include increased thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision, and slow-healing wounds.

Risk factors include obesity, lack of physical activity, family history, and age.

Management includes lifestyle changes, blood sugar monitoring, and medication.
